25 # adi_driver [![Build Status](https://travis-ci.org/tork-a/adi_driver.svg?branch=master)](https://travis-ci.org/tork-a/adi_driver)
26 
27 This package contains ROS driver nodes for Analog Devices(ADI) sensor
28 products mainly communicate by SPI(Serial Periferal Interface).
29 
30 Currently supported devices are:
31 
32 - [ADIS16470](http://www.analog.com/en/products/mems/inertial-measurement-units/adis16470.html)
33  - Wide Dynamic Range Mini MEMS IMU
34 
35 - [ADXL345](http://www.analog.com/en/products/mems/accelerometers/adxl345.html):
36  - 3-Axis, ±2 g/±4 g/±8 g/±16 g Digital Accelerometer
37  - The support for this device is experimental
38 
39 You need a SPI interface on your PC to communicate with device. This
40 package supports
41 [Devantech's USB-IIS](https://www.robot-electronics.co.uk/htm/usb_iss_tech.htm)
42 as the USB-SPI converter.
43 
44 ## USB-IIS
45 
46 ### Overview
47 
48 <div align="center">
49  <img src="doc/USB-ISS.jpg" width="60%"/>
50 </div>
51 
52 [USB-IIS](https://www.robot-electronics.co.uk/htm/usb_iss_tech.htm) is
53 a USB to Serial/I2C/SPI converter, simple, small and easy to use. You
54 don't need any extra library like libusb or libftdi. The device is
55 available on /dev/ttyACM* as modem device.
56 
57 Please consult the
58 [product information](https://www.robot-electronics.co.uk/htm/usb_iss_tech.htm)
59 and
60 [SPI documentation](https://www.robot-electronics.co.uk/htm/usb_iss_spi_tech.htm)
61 for the detail.
62 
63 ### Tips
64 
65 You need to remove the jumper block on ``Power link`` pins to provide
66 3.3V for the device.
67 
68 You need to add your user to dialout group to acces /dev/ttyACM* .
69 
70 ``` $ sudo adduser your_user_name dialout ```
71 
72 If it takes several seconds until /dev/ttyACM* available, you need to
73 uninstall modemmanager as:
74 
75 ``` $ sudo apt remove modemmanager ```

77 ## ADIS16470
78 
79 ### Overview
80 
81 [ADIS16470](http://www.analog.com/en/products/mems/inertial-measurement-units/adis16470.html)
82 is a complete inertial system that includes a triaxis gyroscope and a
83 triaxis accelerometer.
84 
85 <div align="center">
86  <img src="doc/ADIS16470_Breakout.jpg" width="60%"/>
87 </div>
88 
89 You can use
90 [Breakout board](http://www.analog.com/en/design-center/evaluation-hardware-and-software/evaluation-boards-kits/EVAL-ADIS16470.html)
91 for easy use.
92 
93 ### Connection
94 
95 <div align="center">
96  <img src="doc/ADIS16470_Connection.jpg" width="60%"/>
97 </div>
98 
99 You need to build a flat cable to connect the USB-ISS and the
100 ADIS16470 breakout board. The picture shows a implementation.
101 
102 Very simple schematic is here. J1 is the USB-ISS pin and J2 is the 2mm
103 pin headers on the ADIS16470 breakout board.
104 
105 <div align="center">
106  <img src="doc/ADIS16470_Cable.png" width="60%"/>
107 </div>
108 
109 Note: you only need to connect one of the power-line(3.3V and
110 GND). They are connected in the breakout board.
111 
112 ### BOM
113 - J1: 2550 Connector 6pin
114  - Available at [Akiduki](http://akizukidenshi.com/catalog/g/gC-12155/)
115 - J2: FCI Connector for 1.0mm pitch ribon cables
116  - Available at [RS Components](https://jp.rs-online.com/web/p/idc-connectors/6737749/)
117 - 1.0 mm pitch ribon cable
118  - Available at [Aitendo](http://www.aitendo.com/product/11809)
119 
120 ### Quick start
121 
122 Connect your sensor to USB port. Run the launch file as:
123 
124 ``` $ roslaunch adi_driver adis16470.launch ```
125 
126 You can see the model of ADIS16470 breakout board in rviz panel.
